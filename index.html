What it does

A memory that reasons about which version of a fact is true

Value-ranked recall

Retrieval by relevance × accrued value, per-type decay, and a lexical+semantic auto-mode (BM25 + embeddings fused by RRF). Zero-dep by default.

Correction & erasure

revert(), retract_lineage(), echo_guard, forget_subject() — undo, cascade a lineage-aware retraction, prove deletion with a signed tombstone.

Tamper-evident receipts

verify_writes() over a hash-chained, optionally Ed25519-signed log — plus anchor(), a Certificate-Transparency-style witness that catches a key-holder rewrite.

Poison-resistant influence

recall(influence_only=True) restricts what may drive an action to corroborated memories — drops a single-instance retrieval-poisoning hijack toward 0%.

Cross-store erasure audit

DeletionManifest + ErasureAuditor adversarially re-check whether a "deleted" subject is still reconstructible — in the vector index, cache, and logs, not just the row.

Framework adapters

Drop-in backends for OpenAI Agents, AutoGen, LangGraph, LlamaIndex, Google ADK, and Pydantic AI — each carrying the current-truth recall differentiator.

Governance & erasure

Deletion you can actually verify

"Delete the row and log it" isn't erasure — the same content survives in the vector index (as a reconstructible embedding), caches, and logs. mnemo ships the primitives to make correction and deletion tamper-evident, authenticated, and cross-store auditable — and is honest about what an in-store receipt can't prove.

CT-style anchor

anchor() / verify_consistency() — an external signed tree head (RFC 6962) that catches an operator who holds the key and rewrites history. Bare hash chains can't.

Authenticated erasure

forget_subject(authorized_by=…, basis=…) binds a deletion to a principal's Ed25519 signature and records the decision basis — inside the tamper-evident tombstone.

Reconstruction audit

The ErasureAuditor re-attempts recovery from surviving vectors after a delete — the "is it still reconstructible?" check DSAR tooling skips. A lower bound; not a proof of destruction.

Honest scope in SECURITY.md: no RCE/ReDoS surface (JSON-only, no pickle/eval), constant-time secret comparison — and two documented residuals (unpinned-signature footgun, opt-in resource caps). A first internal security pass, not a formal pentest.
